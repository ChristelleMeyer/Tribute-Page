Ferdinand de Saussure

Father of modern linguistics


Biography


1857:
Ferdinand de Saussure was born November 26th, 1857 in Geneva, Switzerland. At a very young age, Saussure's intellectual capacities were already quite noticable. Through his studies at the University of Geneva, the University of Leipzig, and the University of Berlin; he was able to demonstrate an exceptional comprehension of languages (Sanskrit, Latin, Ancient Greek) and their systems.



1878:
After only a two year attendance at the University of Leipzig, Saussure published Mémoire sur le système primitif des voyelles dans les langues indo-européennes.




1880:
Saussure was awarded his doctorate after he defended his dissertation on De l'emploi du génitif absolu en Sanscrit..



1892:
After occupying teaching positions at the University of Paris and at l'École pratique des hautes études, Saussure was offered a tenure in 1892 at the University of Geneva, where is lectures would include but not be limited to subjects such has the study of Indo-European languages and Sanskrit.




1907:
He started his lectures on Course of General Linguistics./His three lectures (1907-1911) are now the bases and fundamentals of language and the study of signs in languages as we know them today. These lectures notes were later gathered and published as Cours de linguistique générale in 1916 by some of his students, after he past.



1913:
He died on february 22nd, 1913 in Vufflens-le-Château, Switzerland.
